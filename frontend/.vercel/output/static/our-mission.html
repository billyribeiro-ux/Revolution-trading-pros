The Institutional Lexicon

We speak the language of the market. You will too.

Expectancy

The average amount you can expect to win (or lose) per dollar at risk over N trades.

R-Multiple

A standardized unit of risk. We measure success in R units, not dollars.

Auction Theory

The market is a two-way auction process seeking to facilitate trade and find fair value.

Alpha Decay

The reduction in edge effectiveness as more participants exploit the same inefficiency.

Liquidity

The ability to enter or exit a position without significant price impact (slippage).

Variance

The statistical deviation from expected results. The 'luck' factor in the short term.

Absorption

When aggressive buying/selling is met with passive limit orders, halting price movement.

Delta

The difference between buying volume and selling volume at a specific price node.

Gamma

The rate of change of Delta. Crucial for understanding options dealer hedging flows.

Vanna

The sensitivity of Delta to changes in Implied Volatility.

Dark Pools

Private exchanges for trading securities that are not accessible by the investing public.

Drawdown

The peak-to-trough decline during a specific record period of an investment.
