The Truth About Technical Indicators

Most new traders fail because they treat indicators as "Go/Stop" signals. They buy when the RSI crosses 30, or sell when lines cross, without understanding context.

Here is the reality: Indicators are just derivatives of price. They are tools, not crystal balls. In our community, we teach you to use these tools to build a case—a "confluence" of evidence—that tilts the probabilities in your favor.

  • Stop chasing "lagging" signals blindly.
  • Start using indicators to confirm price action.
  • Learn to spot what the institutions are doing.

The Power of Confluence

A single indicator can be wrong. Three indicators telling the same story are rarely wrong. This is how we find high-probability trades.

1

Trend

We use Moving Averages to determine if the market is bullish or bearish. We never fight the trend.

2

Location

We wait for price to return to value areas like VWAP or Support zones. We don't chase extended moves.

3

Momentum

We execute when RSI or MACD confirms the buyers are stepping back in. This creates the "sniper" entry.
